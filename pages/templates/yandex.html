Yandex

A field guide to advanced search-operator reconnaissance on Yandex

Yandex's syntax diverges the most from the Google model — it uses proximity operators instead of simple AND logic, and its crawler indexes Russian-language and CIS-region content more thoroughly than Western engines typically do.

Authorized use only

02 / Illustrative Patterns

Common reconnaissance categories

Categories most public OSINT guides reference for Yandex specifically — shown at the pattern level so you understand the intent behind each.

host:target.ru mime:xlsx "конфиденциально"

Combines the host-wide operator with a file-type and sensitivity-marker search across an entire domain's subdomains.

title:"индекс каталога" site:target.ru

Yandex's equivalent of the classic directory-listing pattern, phrased for Russian-language server defaults.

"логин /2 пароль" site:target.ru

Proximity search for "login" occurring within two words of "password" — syntax with no direct Google equivalent.

url:admin site:target.ru mime:php

Looks for admin-panel paths on PHP-driven pages within a specific domain, for authorized infrastructure review.

mime:pdf site:.ru "не для распространения"

Finds PDF files carrying a "not for distribution" marker in Russian, indexed despite the restriction.

host:target.ru "ошибка && mysql"

Combines the host operator with a same-page match for an error term and a database name — surfacing verbose error pages.

03 / Pro Tips

Getting good results

01

Lean on Yandex for CIS-region and Cyrillic content

Its crawler and ranking are tuned for Russian-language material that Western engines under-index — don't assume absence on Google means absence everywhere.

02

Master the proximity operator

/n is one of the most powerful tools here — it finds terms near each other without requiring an exact phrase match.

03

Use host: instead of site: for full-domain sweeps

site: matches only the exact subdomain given; host: automatically includes every subdomain underneath it.

04

Mind the difference between & and &&

Single ampersand requires same-sentence proximity; double ampersand only requires same-page — pick based on how tightly related the terms should be.

05

Search in the target language, not just transliterated English

Native-language queries return dramatically more relevant results on Yandex than English keywords do for Russian-hosted content.

06

Run dorks against your own org first

The highest-value use of these patterns is auditing your own domain's exposure, especially if you operate infrastructure in the region.

04 / Scope & Ethics

Where the line is

Read before you search
  • Authorization matters, especially for host: sweeps. Full-domain reconnaissance is appropriate for auditing your own organization, not for probing a third party without permission.
  • Finding ≠ accessing. Discovering an exposed file or admin path is different from downloading or logging into anything you find.
  • Report, don't exploit. If you stumble on someone else's real exposed data, the responsible move is good-faith disclosure, not further probing.
  • Respect site terms and regional law. Automated querying at scale can violate a site's terms of service even where each individual query is legitimate.
