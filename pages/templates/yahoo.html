🟣 Yahoo

Yahoo Search runs on a smaller, less consistently-honored operator set than Google, partly because results are blended with Bing under the hood.

Unreliable on Yahoo: filetype: support has degraded over the years and is best-effort rather than guaranteed. There's no reliable date-range operator, and allintext/allinurl/allintitle/inanchor/related/cache have no equivalent.

Filters

FilterWhat it does
intitle:Finds pages with the keyword in the page title
inurl:Searches for URLs containing the specified keyword
site:Restricts results to a specific website or domain
link:Finds pages that link to a specific URL
filetype:Best-effort file type filter (not always honored)

Operators

OperatorWhat it does
"phrase"Exact phrase match
ORMatches either term
-Excludes a term
()Groups operators for combined logic

Sample Dorks

Domain-restricted search
site:example.com "contact us"

Finds contact pages on a specific domain.

Title match
intitle:"John Doe" "profile"

Finds profile-style pages with a name in the title.

Inbound links
link:example.com -site:example.com

Finds pages elsewhere on the web linking to a target domain.

When to use Yahoo dorking

Third opinion / redundancy — occasionally surfaces older or smaller indexed pages that Google and Bing have dropped or deprioritized.
Legacy web content — Yahoo's index has historically retained older pages longer in some niches.
Cross-checking link: results — useful as a secondary source when researching backlinks, since Google deprecated its own link: reliability years ago.
