🦆 DuckDuckGo

DuckDuckGo shares a Google-like operator subset but is noticeably smaller - several advanced Google operators simply aren't honored here.

Not supported on DuckDuckGo: allintext:, allinurl:, allintitle:, inanchor:, allinpostauthor:, related:, cache:, numrange:, and after:/before: date filtering. Don't rely on these here even though they work on Google.

Filters

FilterWhat it does
intext:Finds pages containing the keyword anywhere in the page text
intitle:Finds pages with the keyword in the page title
inurl:Searches for URLs containing the specified keyword
site:Restricts results to a specific website or domain
filetype:Limits results to a specific file type (pdf, docx, xls, etc.)

Operators

OperatorWhat it does
"phrase"Searches for the exact phrase as written
ORReturns results matching either term
-Excludes results containing the specified term or site
+Forces inclusion of a term
*Wildcard for unknown words or patterns
()Groups operators to control query logic

Sample Dorks

Exposed docs
site:example.com filetype:pdf "confidential"

Finds PDF files on a specific domain containing the word "confidential".

Login pages
site:example.com inurl:login OR inurl:admin

Finds indexed login or admin panel pages on a target domain.

Title + phrase combo
intitle:"John Doe" "resume"

Finds pages with a name in the title alongside the word "resume" in the body.
